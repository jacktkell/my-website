About Me

My name is Jack Kelling. Although I am enrolled in the DC course, I am taking advantage of the online delivery as I live in Panama City, Florida. I've been here for 8 years but I've also lived in Alabama where I went to Troy University pursuing my Bachelor's in Literature. I dropped out just before Covid-19 became the major force that it is because I was unhappy with the state the English department was in and was doubting if teaching was the career for me. Prior to Troy and Panama City, my family has lived in Texas, Colorado, and Iowa.

Why I Want To Be A Software Engineer

My whole life I've been told that any career in technology is the way to go. It's always expanding, always hiring, pays well, and contributes to society. That's all well and good, but I imagined it as a boring desk job and ruled it out as a possibility for years. It was only recently as I was changing my mind about teaching that my brother got a job as programmer and liked it so much that he got his wife into it. And she liked it so much that she quit nursing to pursue it full time. Obviously, I had misjudged the field. After talking to them about how challenging and engaging the work can be I bought a Java course on Udemy and fell for it. I decided the proper way to learn everything I could and get a job was to follow in their steps and enroll with Flatiron, so here I am.

My Hobbies

If you hadn't guessed by the fact that I devoted three years of study and too much money to the subject, I am an avid reader. I think when I truly fell in love with reading was at school when I was, at a guess, in sixth grade. My eyes were drawn to a thick hardback with a circle made from flowing cursive looking characters and in the center of the circle was an eye. I went to check it out and the librarian warned me it was probably too advanced for my reading level. I took to the challenge and found my favorite book - The Lord of the Rings. I've gone through hiatuses where I won't have the drive to read or can't seem to finish books, but then there's always one that rekindles the passion. Currently that book is 11/22/63 by Stephen King, who is simultaneously one of my favorite authors and among my most loathed authors.
